About

As someone that is deeply interested in artistic expression, writing is my favorite medium and one closest to my heart. Having found writing very early on in my life, writing has almost always been a big part of my life as a means of emotional and artistic expression.

Writing has always been for me a useful means of self discovery and creativity; a means of creative exploration and maintaining an inner and outward intellectual struggle in pursuit of truth, objectivity and beauty.

I developed an interest in writing in my early teens: keeping a journal, making up stories, and writing dialogue were things that I really enjoyed. Later on, I would have a short career in graphic designing which I really enjoyed for the time it lasted (something that I still enjoy doing) but would eventually move on to wanting to become a writer. Content writing is fun and I'm really good at it. I want to be eventually able to run my own blog/site and write some books about philosophy and if lucky enough, also be a part of an indie movie.

Writer & Content Manager.

My content writing journey started in 2019. My first job was for a quite well-established site called Yoreoyster, I wrote for them extensively on a wide range of topics. The position was quite demanding, it required long hours of work (most of which was spent researching/looking for data; sometimes data that wasn't even available on the web which would result in having to reach out to service providers), quick turnarounds, and a very competitive work environment. My time there taught me a lot about teamwork and detailed-oriented research and writing.

I've also worked as a content manager for DadsAgree and SimplyBestOf. My time there taught me a lot about some of the long-term planning that goes into content creation: the research it requires to form a content strategy and the creativity to utilize it. Looking for and identifying new content opportunities, creating/updating content guidelines, monitoring website traffic, content promotion: managing marketing budgets, updating/improving existing content, and supervising/editing content are just some of the things that go overlooked when we think of the subject of content creation and maintaining a website.

I've also worked for Globetrotter Polar Bears and as a script writer for Promoshin.

Why You Should Hire Me?

In essence: An extraordinary audience awareness and the ability to influence, persuade while at the time offering credible content illustrated in an interesting way are just some of the things I can offer to do for you.

Skills

Here's a list of some of the things I can do for you.


SEO Optimization: I write detail-oriented content that's SEO-friendly and fun to read, focused more on hard work and autheticity rather keyword stuffing and other such practices.


Research: Getting familiar with a topic is the first and often most interesting part of writing. Collecting data, turning it into a story / something fun to read is always an adventure. The interesting thing about research is that a well-researched piece of content will stand out while content that's merely put together rarely has legs to stand on.


Editing and Revisions: Once I'm done with the writing part of the process, I continue to go over my work multiple times to make suer that everything's in order. I start off with general proofreading to eliminate any stupid mistakes (such as typos, punctuation erorrs etc. ) that may be floating out there and continue to line editing to improve the general readability and flow of the content; ensuring adherence to client's style guide, tone and voice of content. And a final overview to ensure appropriate use of targeted keywords, fact checking, proper citation/sourcing, presence of relevant internal and external links etc. This helps ensure I offer content that's ready to publish upon submission, saving both myself and my client a lot of time.


Researching Keywords: A big part of being a content manager is keyword research. Finding a good keyword is a quest that’s rarely straightforward, something that requires counter intuitive thinking and creativity. Searching for keywords, performing keyword analysis, analyzing the keywords competitors are ranking for, narrowing down a list of seed keywords, extracting a list of more specified long tail keywords to tackle are just some of the aspects of keyword research that any good keyword researcher must be closely familiar with.


Graphic Designing: Although I’m not a professional graphic designer, graphic designing is something that I really enjoy doing. If there’s a subject I’m interested in after writing, it’s communicating through color/sensory visual cues. Even to my writing, I would say, there is a certain visual aspect that’s kind of hard to ignore.

Services and Rates

I write content that's fun to read and keeps the reader engaged. I have tons of experience with SEO-optimized content and use that to craft well-organized, authoritative, data-driven content that ranks well.

The content I produce is edited, arranged/ordered multiple times beforehand, making sure I offer publish-ready content upon submission. I pay specific attention to make sure I adapt to the tone and voice of your brand; making sure your brand values are being upheld and your ideas are being communicated correctly.

All the content I write is produced by me and me alone. I don't outsource any of my work and give complete attention to any and all the work I'm hired for.

My freelance content writing rates are based on the approximate length of the project, the amount of research required, and the required turnaround time. In my experience deciding upon a price beforehand works really well and is how I generally like to do business.

I do not charge a per word / per hour fee, something that I believe is generally prone to tipping the balance either one way or the other and can lead to confusion. Having to keep track of every and, the and if is not only tedious but can also negatively affect the quality of writing. Writing good content for a fixed price is something that I believe works out much more smoothly.
